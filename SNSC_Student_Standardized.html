Student Home

What You Are Doing

You will follow information connected to this course, read a physical or replica publication, keep a notebook by hand, and use the five moves to slow down your own reading. The goal is not to become cynical. The goal is to become more specific, more patient, and more useful as a reader.

Your Publication

Bring Something With Editorial Intention

Your instructor will tell you what counts for this course. It may be a newspaper, a magazine, a trade publication, a printed academic journal, or a digital replica edition that preserves the visual structure of print.

Good choices

A local paper, Sunday paper, magazine issue, field journal, campus library replica edition, PressReader edition, or trade publication.

What matters

You should be able to see hierarchy, placement, surrounding stories, images, captions, ads, and what an editor chose to put nearby.

The Notebook

Your Weekly Practice

Notebook entry · by hand

What I noticed: one specific observation from the publication or thread.

What moved: what changed in the story, coverage, source chain, or public conversation.

What I am sitting with: one question that remains unresolved.

Photograph the entry and upload it to Canvas when assigned. The notebook is not busywork. It is the fieldwork that makes your final thinking possible.
