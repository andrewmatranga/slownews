Assessment Home

Assessment Philosophy

Assessment rewards the move, not the performance of sounding informed. A student earns credit by making specific observations, tracing evidence, comparing sources, applying field knowledge, and revising public-facing thought.

Core grading question

Did the student do the specific move this assignment requires, or did they substitute something adjacent?

Notebook + Discussion

Holistic Assessment

The notebook and discussion work should be assessed for practice, development, and specificity rather than polished prose. A complete notebook shows a developing habit of attention. A strong discussion contribution gives the room something to work with: an example, question, comparison, or complication.

LevelDescription
Complete and engagedAll entries present; observations become more specific over time; discussion posts prepare the student for class.
Mostly completeMinor gaps, but the practice is visible and useful.
PartialRepeatedly thin, late, generic, or assembled near the deadline.
InsufficientToo little evidence of sustained practice to assess.
Feedback Language

Reusable Instructor Comments

Observation

“Strong specificity here. You stayed close to the example before interpreting it.”

“Slow down and document what is actually present before moving to conclusions.”

Evidence

“The claim is clear, but the source path needs more documentation.”

“What would a reader need to verify before accepting this?”

Interpretation

“This becomes stronger when your field knowledge does the interpretive work.”

“What can you see here that a general reader would miss?”

Revision

“Your next draft should narrow the claim and add one more specific example.”

“The strongest part of this is the question you discovered. Build from there.”
